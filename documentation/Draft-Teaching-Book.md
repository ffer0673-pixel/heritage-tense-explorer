### **Introduction: Why Grammar Meets Heritage**
Language does not exist in a vacuum; it is a living, breathing tool used to share culture, history, and daily experience. The *Tenses Around Us* methodology is built on two core principles:
1. **Contextual Learning:** Cognitive studies show that grammar is retained far more effectively when connected to vivid narratives rather than dry, abstract sentences.
2. **Cultural Preservation:** By learning English using sentences about local landmarks, students practice their language skills while building pride and awareness of Indonesian history, specifically the unique Sundanese-Chinese-Betawi fusion of Tangerang (Cina Benteng).

---

### **Tangerang Local Wisdom: The Cultural Foundation**
This book is anchored on five key cultural fragments of Tangerang:
* **🏛️ Benteng Heritage:** The historic Cina-Benteng quarter and the oldest museum in Tangerang housed in a restored 17th-century structure.
* **🏮 Pasar Lama:** Tangerang's oldest market and culinary epicentre—a buzzing mix of street food, heritage shops, and lantern-lit nights.
* **🌊 Sungai Cisadane:** The river that shaped the city's trade routes, now hosting promenades, sunset markets, and the iconic Jembatan Berendeng.
* **🍜 Kuliner Tangerang:** A distinct culinary fusion of Sundanese, Chinese, and Betawi flavors, famous for Laksa Tangerang, sayur besan, and dodol.
* **🎎 Budaya Cina Benteng:** A unique Peranakan community that has called Tangerang home for over five centuries.

## **Part 1: The Present Tense Family**

### **Chapter 1: Simple Present Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Present Tense / Waktu Sekarang Sederhana
* **Overview:** Used for habits, general truths, routines, and permanent situations—perfect for describing daily life and structures around Tangerang.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + V1 (s/es) + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + do/does + not + V1 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Do/Does + S + V1 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Don't/Doesn't + S + V1 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Daily Habits / Routines:** Action repeated regularly.
2. **General Facts:** Timeless truths or geographical facts.
3. **Scheduled Events:** Timetable occurrences.
4. **Time Expressions:** *every day, always, usually, often, sometimes, never, on Sundays.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I walk along the Cisadane River every morning.
* *Negative:* I do not visit Museum Benteng on Mondays.
* *Interrogative:* Do you live in Tangerang?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Don't you live in Tangerang?
* *Fact:* Tangerang sits on the banks of the Cisadane River.

#### **Reading Corner (Excerpt from "Kuliner Tangerang")**
> "Tangerang's culinary heritage tells the story of centuries of cultural fusion. Every bowl of Laksa Tangerang combines local Sundanese rice noodles with Chinese spices and Betawi style gravy."

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Simple Present?
   * A. The vendor cooked laksa yesterday.
   * B. The vendor is cooking laksa right now.
   * C. The vendor cooks laksa every morning.
   * D. The vendor will cook laksa tomorrow.
   * *Answer: C. Simple Present uses V1 (+s/es) for habits.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "Mei ________ (not/live) near the Cisadane River."
   * *Answer: does not live*

### **Chapter 2: Present Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Present Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Sekarang
* **Overview:** Used for actions in progress right now or temporary situations happening around the present moment.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + am/is/are + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + am/is/are + not + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Aren't/Isn't + S + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Actions happening right now:** Ongoing at the moment of speaking.
2. **Temporary situations:** Continuous but not permanent.
3. **Time Expressions:** *now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* She is buying dodol at Pasar Lama.
* *Negative:* I am not walking to the museum at the moment.
* *Interrogative:* Is she taking photos at Pasar Lama?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Aren't you visiting Benteng Heritage today?
* *Scene:* The Cisadane River is flowing faster than usual today.

#### **Reading Corner (Excerpt from "Sore di Cisadane")**
> "The sun is setting behind the rooftops of Pasar Lama. I am walking slowly along the Cisadane promenade, watching the orange light spill across the water."

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Identify the Present Continuous sentence:
   * A. The dancers practice every day.
   * B. The dancers are practicing the barongsai right now.
   * C. The dancers practiced last night.
   * D. The dancers will practice tomorrow.
   * *Answer: B. "are practicing" represents an action in progress.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "We ________ (watch) the sunset over the river at this moment."
   * *Answer: are watching*

### **Chapter 3: Present Perfect Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Present Perfect Tense / Waktu Selesai Sekarang
* **Overview:** Used for actions completed at an unspecified time before now, where the focus is on the result or experience affecting the present.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + have/has + V3 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + have/has + not + V3 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Have/Has + S + V3 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Haven't/Hasn't + S + V3 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Past actions with present impact:** The results are still visible or relevant.
2. **Life experiences:** What someone has or has not done in their lifetime.
3. **Unfinished time periods:** Action starting in the past and continuing now.
4. **Time Expressions:** *already, yet, just, ever, never, since, for, recently.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I have visited Museum Benteng three times.
* *Negative:* She has not tried sayur besan before.
* *Interrogative:* Has she tried dodol Tangerang yet?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Haven't you been to Pasar Lama before?
* *Context:* Many Cina Benteng families have preserved their traditions for generations.

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Present Perfect?
   * A. She has been painting the temple.
   * B. She has painted the temple.
   * C. She paints the temple.
   * D. She was painting the temple.
   * *Answer: B. have/has + V3 (painted).*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "Pak Tan ________ (live) in Tangerang since 2010."
   * *Answer: has lived*

### **Chapter 4: Present Perfect Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Present Perfect Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Selesai Sekarang
* **Overview:** Used for actions that started in the past and are still continuing, or have just finished, with an emphasis on duration.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + have/has + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + have/has + not + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Have/Has + S + been + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Haven't/Hasn't + S + been + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Focus on duration:** Emphasizing how long an action has been happening.
2. **Action still in progress:** Started in the past and continues into the present.
3. **Time Expressions:** *for, since, lately, recently, all day, all morning.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I have been studying the history of Benteng Heritage for two hours.
* *Negative:* He has not been working at the stall today.
* *Interrogative:* Have they been cleaning the Cisadane riverbank?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Hasn't it been raining in Tangerang all day?
* *Context:* The community has been preserving Cap Go Meh traditions for centuries.

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Select the Present Perfect Continuous form:
   * A. I have baked cake all morning.
   * B. I have been baking cake since dawn.
   * C. I was baking cake.
   * D. I bake cake on weekends.
   * *Answer: B. "have been baking" highlights the continuous duration.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "The vendors ________ (sell) laksa at Pasar Lama for years."
   * *Answer: have been selling*

## **Part 2: The Past Tense Family**

### **Chapter 5: Simple Past Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Tense / Waktu Lampau Sederhana
* **Overview:** Used for completed actions that took place at a specific, defined time in the past.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + V2 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + did + not + V1 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Did + S + V1 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Didn't + S + V1 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Completed past actions:** Action started and finished in the past.
2. **Series of past actions:** Successive events.
3. **Time Expressions:** *yesterday, last week, last year, ago, in 2020, when I was young.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I visited Benteng Heritage last weekend.
* *Negative:* I did not eat laksa yesterday.
* *Interrogative:* Did you visit Pasar Lama yesterday?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Didn't they restore the old building in 2019?
* *History:* The Dutch built the original fort by the Cisadane in the 17th century.

#### **Reading Corner (Excerpt from "Kisah Pasar Lama")**
> "Pak Tan opened the wooden shutters of his shop and smiled. 'When my grandfather built this place in 1925,' he said, 'the road had not yet been paved.'"

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Simple Past?
   * A. The captain ride the boat.
   * B. The captain rode the boat yesterday.
   * C. The captain has ridden the boat.
   * D. The captain will ride the boat.
   * *Answer: B. rode is the V2 form of ride.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "Our family ________ (try) sayur besan at a wedding last month."
   * *Answer: tried*

### **Chapter 6: Past Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Lampau
* **Overview:** Used for actions that were actively in progress at a specific moment in the past, or interrupted by another event.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + was/were + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + was/were + not + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Was/Were + S + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Wasn't/Weren't + S + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Action in progress in the past:** Ongoing at a specific past hour.
2. **Interrupted past actions:** A continuous past action cut short by a sudden simple past action (using *when* or *while*).
3. **Time Expressions:** *while, when, as, all day yesterday, at 5 pm yesterday.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I was crossing the Cisadane bridge when it started raining.
* *Negative:* He was not eating at the stall.
* *Interrogative:* Were they preparing the festival decorations?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Wasn't he learning to play angklung last month?
* *Description:* The barongsai was performing while the crowd cheered.

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Identify the Past Continuous sentence:
   * A. I was visiting the museum at noon yesterday.
   * B. I visited the museum yesterday.
   * C. I had visited the museum before.
   * D. I will be visiting the museum.
   * *Answer: A. "was visiting" shows ongoing action in the past.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "The vendors ________ (close) their stalls when the heavy storm began."
   * *Answer: were closing*

### **Chapter 7: Past Perfect Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Perfect Tense / Waktu Selesai Lampau
* **Overview:** Used for an action completed before another past action—often referred to as the "past of the past."

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + had + V3 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + had + not + V3 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Had + S + V3 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Hadn't + S + V3 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Sequence of two past events:** Clarifies which past action happened first.
2. **Time Expressions:** *before, after, by the time, already, just, by 2020.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I had visited Benteng Heritage before I moved to Tangerang.
* *Negative:* He had not tried sayur besan before the wedding.
* *Interrogative:* Had they finished the parade before the storm?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Hadn't you been to the museum before that day?
* *Context:* By 1900, traders had already established Pasar Lama as a commercial hub.

#### **Reading Corner (Excerpt from "Rumah Tua Benteng")**
> "Mei had not seen the family house for twenty years. She thought the roof would have collapsed and the garden would have disappeared. But the house was still there. Someone had painted the walls a soft cream."

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Past Perfect?
   * A. We finished the tour when the museum closed.
   * B. We had finished the tour before the museum closed.
   * C. We have finished the tour.
   * D. We were finishing the tour.
   * *Answer: B. "had finished" represents the action that completed first.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "She ________ (eat) laksa before the rain started."
   * *Answer: had eaten*

### **Chapter 8: Past Perfect Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Perfect Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Selesai Lampau
* **Overview:** Used for ongoing actions that started and continued up to a specific point of time in the past before another past action occurred.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + had + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + had + not + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Had + S + been + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Hadn't + S + been + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Duration up to a past event:** Focus on how long the first action lasted before the second past action took place.
2. **Time Expressions:** *for, since, before, until, by the time.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I had been walking along the Cisadane for an hour before it rained.
* *Negative:* He had not been working there for long.
* *Interrogative:* Had they been cleaning the riverbank all week?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Hadn't she been teaching at the heritage school?
* *Context:* The community had been preparing Cap Go Meh for weeks before the parade.

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Select the Past Perfect Continuous sentence:
   * A. They were cleaning the temple for hours.
   * B. They had been cleaning the temple for hours before the opening.
   * C. They cleaned the temple yesterday.
   * D. They have been cleaning the temple.
   * *Answer: B. "had been cleaning" with past reference "before the opening".*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "She ________ (sell) laksa for years before she retired."
   * *Answer: had been selling*

## **Part 3: The Future Tense Family**

### **Chapter 9: Simple Future Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Future Tense / Waktu Yang Akan Datang Sederhana
* **Overview:** Used for actions that will happen in the future—including predictions, decisions, promises, and plans.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + will + V1 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + will + not + V1 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Will + S + V1 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Won't + S + V1 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Predictions:** Speculating on future outcomes.
2. **Spontaneous decisions:** Deciding at the moment of speaking.
3. **Plans/Promises:** Stating future intents.
4. **Time Expressions:** *tomorrow, next week, next year, soon, in 2030.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I will visit Benteng Heritage next weekend.
* *Negative:* I will not travel to Tangerang next month.
* *Interrogative:* Will you join us at Pasar Lama tomorrow?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Won't she try sayur besan next week?
* *Context:* Tangerang will welcome more tourists next year.

#### **Reading Corner (Excerpt from "Festival Cap Go Meh")**
> "Next Friday, Pasar Lama will become a river of red lanterns. The barongsai troupe will perform along the main street, and the temple drums will echo all the way to the Cisadane."

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Simple Future?
   * A. The artists paint a mural.
   * B. The artists will paint a mural tomorrow.
   * C. The artists are painting a mural.
   * D. The artists painted a mural.
   * *Answer: B. "will paint" indicates future action.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "The boat ________ (leave) the Cisadane pier at sunrise tomorrow."
   * *Answer: will leave*

## **Part 4: The Past Future Tense Family**

### **Chapter 13: Past Future Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Future Tense / Waktu Pengandaian Lampau
* **Overview:** Used to describe a future action seen from a past perspective—very common in reported speech, promises, or conditional scenarios.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + would/should + V1 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + would/should + not + V1 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Would/Should + S + V1 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Wouldn't/Shouldn't + S + V1 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Future from a past viewpoint:** What was planned or expected.
2. **Reported Speech:** Changing "will" to "would" when reporting past statements.
3. **Time Expressions:** *the next day, later, the following week, soon after.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I said I would visit Benteng Heritage the next day.
* *Negative:* He claimed he would not move out of Tangerang.
* *Interrogative:* Would she try sayur besan at the wedding?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Wouldn't you visit Pasar Lama with us?
* *Thought:* We knew the museum would close early that day.

#### **Exercises**
1. **Multiple Choice:** Which sentence is in Past Future?
   * A. Andi said he will paint the temple the next day.
   * B. Andi said he would paint the temple the next day.
   * C. Andi says he paints the temple.
   * D. Andi painted the temple yesterday.
   * *Answer: B. "would paint" represents the past future tense.*
2. **Fill in the blank:** "She promised she ________ (buy) laksa for dinner."
   * *Answer: would buy*

### **Chapter 14: Past Future Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Future Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Pengandaian Lampau
* **Overview:** Used to describe a continuous action that would be in progress at a future moment, viewed from a point in the past.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + would + be + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + would + not + be + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Would + S + be + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Wouldn't + S + be + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Ongoing action from a past viewpoint:** Describing progress of future events predicted in the past.
2. **Time Expressions:** *at that time, the next day, that afternoon, all evening.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I said I would be walking along the Cisadane at sunrise.
* *Negative:* They said they would not be staying long.
* *Interrogative:* Would she be cooking sayur besan all afternoon?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Wouldn't they be cleaning the river the next day?

### **Chapter 15: Past Future Perfect Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Future Perfect Tense / Waktu Selesai Pengandaian Lampau
* **Overview:** Used for an action that would have been completed by a future point, seen from the past—often used to express unfulfilled plans or conditional regrets.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + would + have + V3 + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + would + not + have + V3 + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Would + S + have + V3 + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Wouldn't + S + have + V3 + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Unfulfilled events:** Actions that were expected to finish but did not.
2. **Time Expressions:** *by then, by tomorrow, by the next day, before.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I thought I would have visited Benteng Heritage by then.
* *Negative:* She said she would not have learned Mandarin in time.
* *Interrogative:* Would they have finished cleaning the river?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Wouldn't the museum have opened by then?
* *Thought:* By 2025, they thought the riverside would have been transformed.

   * *Answer: B. would + have + V3 (finished).*

### **Chapter 16: Past Future Perfect Continuous Tense**
* **Indonesian Equivalent:** Past Future Perfect Continuous Tense / Waktu Berlangsung Selesai Pengandaian Lampau
* **Overview:** Used for an ongoing action that would have continued up to a future point, viewed from the past, with emphasis on duration.

#### **Formulas**
| Sentence Type | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| **Positive (+)** | `S + would + have + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Negative (-)** | `S + would + not + have + been + V-ing + O` |
| **Interrogative (?)** | `Would + S + have + been + V-ing + O ?` |
| **Negative Interrogative (-?)** | `Wouldn't + S + have + been + V-ing + O ?` |

#### **Grammar & Usage**
1. **Duration from a past perspective:** Highlighting continuous action leading up to a hypothetical point.
2. **Time Expressions:** *by then, for, by the next day.*

#### **Examples in Context**
* *Positive:* I thought I would have been studying tenses for a year by that month.
* *Negative:* They said they would not have been living there for years.
* *Interrogative:* Would she have been teaching for ten years by 2030?
* *Negative Interrogative:* Wouldn't they have been cleaning the river for a month?
* *Context:* By Cap Go Meh, they said the troupe would have been rehearsing for months.

   * *Answer: A. "would have been walking" fits the structure.*
